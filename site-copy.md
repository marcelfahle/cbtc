## 2 · What this actually is

**A training camp, not a spa week. (There's still paella.)**

This is for women who can run — and want to run *mountains*. Real Marina Alta
terrain, coached properly: line choice, foot placement, braking, poles,
fueling, pacing, race planning. Every single run has a job.

**Why only eight?** Because eight is the most people two coaches can genuinely
coach. You will never be a bib number here. You'll be the one whose descent
line Anna is shouting compliments about.

**The rule we're proudest of:** the last runner on every climb gets a coach
beside her. Always. Nobody gets dropped. Nobody gets babysat either — there's
a difference, and good coaches know it.

## 3 · Who it's for

- **Road runners with trail curiosity.** You can hold your own on 15k, but
  rocky descents turn you into a very careful tourist. That's not a fitness
  problem. It's a skills gap, and it closes fast with the right eyes on you.
- **50k and 80k hopefuls.** There's an ultra on your calendar and a quiet
  feeling that you're winging it. Four days from now you'll have a plan
  written down.
- **Northern women who need vertical.** Your trails are under ice until April.
  Ours aren't.

**Who shouldn't come (yet):**
- Complete beginners — be comfortable running 15k first, then apply for
  Edition 2.
- Podium hunters — we promise better skills and better decisions, not miracles.
- Sufferfest tourists — some climbs here are meant to be hiked. That's not
  weakness, that's racecraft.

  stays alive, and we stay reachable until your goal race. The night before

## 5 · Four days, one arc (itinerary)

- **Day 1 · 6–8k easy.** Land, settle into the villa, shake out the flight on
  coastal single-track, kit check, dinner. Then the goals circle: everyone
  says her race and one fear. The coaches answer both.
- **Day 2 · 14–18k technical.** The rocky stuff, coached hard: lines, feet,
  cadence, braking. We film you on the gnarly section — then you and a coach
  watch your tape that evening, one on one. Slightly weird, wildly useful.
  You'll rewatch it the week before your race.
- **Day 3 · The Long Day.** See below. It gets its own section. It's earned it.
- **Day 4 · 6–10k recovery.** Easy scenic loop, mobility, a proper breakfast,
  and the handover: your race notes, printed — plan, fueling numbers,
  technique cues, next twelve weeks. Then the airport, annoyingly.

## 6 · The Long Day

**This edition: Serra de Bèrnia — about 22k, 1,400m of climbing.**

No hero summit here. The Marina Alta doesn't do one big mountain — it
accumulates. Ridge, descent, repeat, for hours, with everything from Days 1
and 2 now happening at effort: fueling on schedule, pacing decisions, poles
when they help.

Somewhere in the middle, twenty minutes where nobody talks. Sounds woo-woo.
Isn't. The last two hours of your ultra will be exactly this quiet, and it's
better to have met that silence before race day.

And when it's done: we drive ten minutes and walk into the Mediterranean.
Seventeen degrees. You'll swear a little. It's non-negotiable and it's the
thing everyone talks about at dinner.

Every edition runs a different Long Day — Bèrnia this time, Benigembla another
year, sometimes a race bib. Each one gets a page in the camp logbook: route,
weather, eight names. Edition 1 is page one. You'll also take the route home —
its elevation profile on a hand-painted local tile. Not for sale. Only earned.

## 11 · FAQ (additions + rewrites)

**"I'm scared I'll be the slowest."**
Someone will be. She gets a coach beside her on every climb and the same
paella at dinner. This camp doesn't do dropped runners — read the rule in
"What this actually is," then apply.

**How fit do I need to be?**
Comfortable running 15k on rolling ground, happy to hike the steep stuff. The
Long Day adapts to the group. This is a real training camp, not a beginner
weekend — but "real" measures seriousness, not speed.

**Do I need trail experience?**
Some helps. If you've got road fitness and rocky ground makes you nervous,
you're exactly who this is for.

**Is it women only?**
Yes — this edition is women-only. Coaches included.

**What if the weather turns?**
We have backup routes, road-to-trail options and storm protocols. Mountain
plans need escape hatches; ours are written down.

**What kit is mandatory?**
Trail shoes, hydration vest, wind shell, sun protection, emergency blanket,
phone, personal meds. Full list (with our honest shoe opinions) arrives before
you fly.
