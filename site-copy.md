## 4 · What happens when you're accepted (timeline section)

You're not booking four days. This is the shape of it:

**Within 48 hours** — a personal reply from Monika or Anna. If it's a yes,
you'll know why.

**Week 1** — a 30-minute call with one of us: your goal race, your history,
your injuries, your shoes (photograph them before you buy new ones). We start
your runner card — one page about you that we keep updating until your race.

**Weeks 1–8** — the group chat opens. A camp-prep layer on top of your normal
training: two focused sessions a week — vertical strength, downhill work, time
on feet. Three group calls: kickoff, fuelling & kit, final logistics. A short
voice note from your coaches every week.

**Around week 4** — a package in the post. The fuelling you'll use on the Long
Day, so your gut trains with it too. There's other stuff in there.

**26–29 November** — camp. A coach meets you at arrivals; the trail talk
starts in the car.

**December → your goal race** — a follow-up call once your legs are back. The
group chat stays open. We stay reachable until your goal race — and the night
before it, you'll hear from us.

## 5 · The Long Day (per-edition route + logbook)

Day three is the Long Day. This edition: [Serra de Bèrnia — ~22 km, 1,400 m of
climbing]. Not one heroic summit — there isn't one here. The Marina Alta works
differently: it accumulates. Everything from days one and two — lines, feet,
poles, fuelling, pacing — at effort, for hours.

Twenty minutes of it, we run in silence. Not a mindfulness exercise; practice.
The last two hours of your race will be exactly this quiet.

The last runner on any climb has a coach beside them. Always. Two of us, eight
of you — the ratio is the point.

When it's done: the sea. Ten minutes away, seventeen degrees, non-negotiable.

Every edition's Long Day is a different line — Bèrnia this time, Benigembla
another, some editions a race bib — and every one goes in the camp logbook:
the route, the weather, the eight names. You take the day's profile home on a
locally made tile. Earned on the day. Not for sale.

## 6 · What's in it (value stack)

- **Two national-team athletes, eight runners, four days.** No guest-athlete
  rotation, no assistant guides. The ratio is the product.
- **Individual video review.** We film you on technical ground on day two and
  sit down with you the same evening, one to one. We found exactly one other
  camp in Europe that offers this.
- **Your race notes, printed.** You leave with a plan, not a certificate: your
  race plan, your fuelling numbers from the Long Day, your technique cues from
  the video, your next twelve weeks.
- **Sports massage** on the evening of the Long Day. Included, not an upsell.
- **Three nights** in the villa, breakfasts, trail snacks, group dinners.
- **Airport transfers** — driven by a coach, on purpose.
- **The eight weeks before and the months after** — see the timeline. The camp
  is the middle of it, not the whole of it.

Assembled separately — that much coaching from athletes at this level, video
analysis, massage, transfers, board — you're past €3,000. We'd rather charge
€1,350 and choose who comes.

Not included: flights, insurance (mandatory), alcohol, race entries.
