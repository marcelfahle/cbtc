The concept

Technical trail running without the performance theatre.

This is a practical trail-running camp for women who want to become calmer, stronger, and more capable in mountain terrain. You run real Marina Alta trails, work on the skills that matter when the ground gets rocky and technical, and leave with clearer habits for race day.

Two athletes, one small room.

Monika brings deep Costa Blanca trail knowledge and has raced for Lithuania at the World Championships. Anna brings current Icelandic national-team credibility, a Laugavegur podium, sub-3 marathon speed, and a teacher's instinct for explaining hard things plainly.

Terrain first, slogans last.

Every run has a job: climbing rhythm, downhill control, endurance pacing, heat management, fueling, or race planning.

Small because small works.

Eight runners keeps the group coachable, safer, and personal. The point is direct feedback, not being another bib in the pack.

The arc

Four days with a clear arc.

Enough structure to make the trip worth the money. Enough space to recover, ask questions, eat well, and come home sharper than you arrived.

Day 16–8 km easy
Arrival, shakeout & kit check

Alicante transfer window, check-in near Denia, an easy coastal trail run, dinner, group goals, and route briefing.

Day 214–18 km
Technical terrain & foot placement

Rocky, technical Marina Alta trails with coached line choice, foot placement, cadence, braking, and confidence on rough ground.

Day 320–26 km
Long trail day & race craft

A longer route with fueling practice, heat management, pacing decisions, poles where useful, and a proper recovery meal. The goal is to learn how to move well for hours.

Day 46–10 km
Recovery run, workshop & departure

Short scenic loop, mobility, breakfast, personal next-race notes, group Q&A with both hosts, and the Alicante transfer window.

Who it's for.

The first cohort stays narrow: women with a running base who want practical trail skills, warm winter terrain, and direct access to athletes who race internationally.

Road runners moving onto trails

You can run, but technical descents, loose ground, poles, and fueling still feel like a second language.

50K and 80K hopefuls

You have a mountain race on the calendar and want a focused training block with honest feedback.

Northern runners needing sun

You want winter or spring vertical without flying to the other side of the world for it.

Who should not come.

The first version gets stronger by saying no cleanly. A narrow retreat is easier to fill than a vague one.

Complete beginners

You should already be able to run 15 km comfortably. This is not a couch-to-trail weekend.

Result hunters

No one can guarantee a podium or a breakthrough race. The promise is better skill and better decisions.

High-volume sufferfest seekers

This is serious work, but the goal is learning. Some climbs are meant to be hiked because that's the smarter mountain decision.

Questions worth answering early.

Serious runners want pace, terrain, rooms, food, insurance, and risk explained before they apply.

How fit do I need to be?+

You should be comfortable running 15 km on rolling terrain and open to hiking steep climbs. The long day can be adapted, but this is not a beginner weekend.

Do I need trail experience?+

Some trail experience helps, but you don't need to be an expert. This is a good fit if you have road fitness and want to move better on rougher ground.

Is it women only?+

Yes. The first cohort is deliberately women-only so the product has a clear room to serve.

What trails will we run?+

Routes are selected from the Denia, Javea, Bernia, Vall de Laguar, and Marina Alta area depending on weather, group level, and permissions.

What if the weather turns?+

There will be lower-risk backup routes, road-to-trail options, workshop swaps, and heat or storm protocols. Mountain plans need escape routes.

Can I come alone?+

Yes. The retreat is designed for solo travelers. Shared rooms are matched by preference where possible.

What kit is mandatory?+

Trail shoes, hydration vest, phone, emergency blanket, wind shell, sun protection, personal medication, and route-specific extras sent before arrival.
