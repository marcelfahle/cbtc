The concept

A training camp, not a spa week. (There's still paella.)

For women who can run — and want to run mountains. Real Marina Alta terrain, coached properly: line choice, foot placement, braking, poles, fueling, race planning. Every single run has a job.

Two athletes, one small room.

Monika has raced Worlds for Lithuania and lives on these trails. Anna is Icelandic national team with a Laugavegur podium and sub-3 marathon speed. Neither has any interest in impressing you — they're here to make you better.

Terrain first, slogans last.

Every run has a job: climbing rhythm, downhill control, endurance pacing, heat management, fueling, or race planning.

Why only eight?

Because eight is the most people two athletes can genuinely coach. You'll never be a bib number here — you'll be the one whose descent line gets shouted compliments.

The arc

Four days with a clear arc.

Enough structure to be worth the money. Enough space to recover, ask everything, eat well, and fly home sharper than you landed.

Day 16–8 km easy
Arrival, shakeout & kit check

Alicante transfer, check-in near Denia, an easy coastal run to shake out the flight, then dinner and the goals circle: everyone names her race and one fear, and we answer both.

Day 214–18 km
Technical terrain & foot placement

The rocky stuff, coached hard: lines, feet, cadence, braking. We film you on the technical section — then you and one of us watch your tape that evening, one on one. Slightly weird, wildly useful.

Day 320–26 km
Long trail day & race craft

The Long Day — it gets its own section below. Fueling on schedule, real pacing decisions, poles where they help, and the answer to whether you can move well for hours. (You can. With snacks.)

Day 46–10 km
Recovery run, workshop & departure

Easy scenic loop, mobility, a proper breakfast, then the handover: your race notes, printed — plan, fueling numbers, technique cues, next twelve weeks. Then the airport, annoyingly.

Day three

The Long Day.

This edition: Serra de Bèrnia — ~22 km, 1,400 m of climbing.

Not one heroic summit — there isn't one here. The Marina Alta works differently: it accumulates. Everything from days one and two — lines, feet, poles, fuelling, pacing — at effort, for hours.

Twenty minutes of silence

Sounds woo-woo. Isn't. The last two hours of your ultra will be exactly this quiet — better to meet that silence before race day.

The last runner has a coach

On any climb, always. Two of us, eight of you — the ratio is the point.

Then the sea

Ten minutes away, seventeen degrees. You'll swear a little. Everyone talks about it at dinner. Non-negotiable.

Every edition's Long Day is a different line — Bèrnia this time, Benigembla another, some editions a race bib — and every one goes in the camp logbook: the route, the weather, the eight names. You take the day's profile home on a locally made tile. Earned on the day. Not for sale.

Questions worth answering early.

The stuff you'd ask us over coffee, answered before you apply.

I'm scared I'll be the slowest.+

Someone will be. She gets one of us beside her on every climb and the same paella at dinner. This camp doesn't do dropped runners.

What can four days actually change?+

How you move on technical ground — descending, braking, and line choice are skills, and skills move fast with expert eyes on eight people. You'll also leave with a race plan, your fueling numbers, and video of your own feet. What four days can't do: make you fitter by Sunday. Fitness takes months — that's what the eight weeks before and the plan you leave with are for. We're careful about which one we promise.

How fit do I need to be?+

Comfortable running 15 km on rolling ground and happy to hike the steep stuff. The Long Day adapts to the group — this is a real training camp, but 'real' measures seriousness, not speed.

Do I need trail experience?+

Some helps. If you've got road fitness and rocky ground makes you nervous, you're exactly who this is for.

Is it women only?+

Yes — this edition is women-only. Coaches included.

What trails will we run?+

Routes are selected from the Denia, Javea, Bernia, Vall de Laguar, and Marina Alta area depending on weather, group level, and permissions.

What if the weather turns?+

There will be lower-risk backup routes, road-to-trail options, workshop swaps, and heat or storm protocols. Mountain plans need escape routes.

Can I come alone?+

Yes — almost everyone does. Shared rooms are matched by preference, and by day two the strangers thing is over.

What kit is mandatory?+

Trail shoes, hydration vest, wind shell, sun protection, emergency blanket, phone, personal meds. The full list — with our honest shoe opinions — arrives before you fly.

Steal our homework: five Marina Alta routes, free. GPX files, water points, which lines actually go, and where people get cliffed.
